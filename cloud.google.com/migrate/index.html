Migrating VMs to Google Cloud Platform

Free VM Migration for Google Cloud Platform

Your Journey to Cloud Platform Made Easy

Moving computing workloads to the cloud can deliver compelling benefits, but the mechanics of data and workload migration can strain limited IT resources. Google partners are available to help with this migration. Google also offers a free self-service migration capability for servers running supported operating systems from either an on-premises machine or a VM in another public cloud platform. Using this service will create a virtual machine instantiated in your GCP project that is a replica of a machine in another environment.

Getting Started With VM Migration

Many companies start their journey to the cloud by shifting some work from on-premises resources.

Cloud Disaster Recovery

In conjunction with our partners, we have developed tools to continuously replicate your applications to the cloud, providing a robust disaster recovery solution. Leave your expensive secondary data centers behind and rely on the cloud to achieve robust disaster recovery. With Recovery Points Objectives that help you restore from a recent state and Recovery Time Objectives that guarantee you’ll get back up and running quickly, you can rest assured that your workloads are resilient.

Test in Production Environments

Replicating your production environment for staging and testing creates unnecessary costs and operational overhead. The economics of cloud computing allow your development teams to work with the resources they need when they need them. Google and its partners have purpose-built solutions for creating dev/test versions of your production environment without the need for large capital expenditures.

Lift and Shift Workloads

Often it makes sense to move an entire workload to the public cloud. Leveraging the cloud can allow you to escape capital intensive refresh cycles. Google has created solutions with its partners that facilitate this type of move. Using our partner’s on-premises discovery and analysis tools will help you understand the cost benefit of moving to the cloud. Our partners’ leading replication technology moves your data and workloads with minimal time and expense.

Self Service or Partner Assisted?

Broadly speaking, there are two approaches to cloud migration: self-service and partner-assisted. Our experience suggests that many of our customers, from small businesses to large enterprises, ultimately find a partnership approach provides a better experience and consumes less time and money.

Migration Phase Assessment Planning Network Configuration Replication
Self Service Internal teams generate maps of application dependencies and network traffic. Internal teams download cost models from public cloud vendors and create complex spreadsheets modelling the cost. Teams estimate the best virtual resources to use. Teams configure network rules to match their internal systems. Teams use GCP’s free migration service. Premium OS licenses are mapped (when possible) and packed with the images.
Partner Assisted Partners deploy agents on the network to map server and network activity. Application clusters are automatically extracted. Partners determine the appropriate machine types to handle existing workloads across multiple public cloud vendors. Partner solutions enable networking to match the existing topology. Partners replicate the virtual machines into the cloud, accounting for licensing transitions.
